# Project Task Summary

## Project Title

Assessment of sky-subtraction effects on DESI emission-line measurements

## Advisor

Yizhou Gu  
Tsung-Dao Lee Institute

## Research Background

Ground-based optical spectroscopy is affected by night-sky background emission. In particular, strong atmospheric emission lines can leave residuals after sky subtraction, which may influence the measurement of astronomical emission lines.

For DESI spectra, such residuals may affect line fluxes, line ratios, and therefore the physical interpretation of galaxy and AGN spectra.

## Current Science Goal

The current task is to evaluate the effect of sky subtraction around the oxygen sky-line region near:

- 6300 Å
- 6364 Å

The focus is on a narrow-line AGN sample in the redshift range:

- z = 0.25–0.29

This project aims to understand whether sky residuals near these wavelengths can bias emission-line measurements and related scientific conclusions.

## Main Questions

1. What are the main night-sky emission features relevant to DESI optical spectroscopy?
2. How do sky residuals near 6300/6364 Å affect emission-line measurements?
3. How can DESI spectra be inspected and prepared for a systematic sky-subtraction assessment?
4. How might these effects influence the interpretation of narrow-line AGN spectra?

## Current Stage

This repository currently represents the setup and exploration stage of the project, including background review, DESI FITS inspection, public spectrum access, and preliminary spectral exploration.
